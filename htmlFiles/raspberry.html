Raspberry Cheesecake

PREP: 35 min

Cook: 2 hours

Servings: 8 Slices

Rating: 5/5

Ingredients

Crust

  • 16 sheets Graham crackers
  • 12 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp Sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Salt

Cheesecake

  • 20 oz Cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup Sugar
  • 1 tbsp Corn starch
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • 1/4 cup Sour cream
  • 2 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 2 Eggs

Whipped Cream

  • 1 1/2 cups Heavy whipping cream
  • Powdered sugarr
  • 1/4 tsp Salt

Raspberry Jam

  • 4 cups Raspberries
  • 1 cup Sugar
  • 2 tbsp Lemon juice

Toppings

  • Zest of half a lemon
  • Fresh mint leaves for decoration

Instructions

Crust

  1. In a large mixing bowl using a hand or stand mixer with the whisk attachment, whisk your cream cheese until smooth.
  2. Add in your sugar, salt, and cornstarch. Whisk until smooth.
  3. Scrape down your bowl, and whisk in your sour cream and vanilla.
  4. Finally, Whisk in your eggs one at a time. Whisk until combined, don't over-mix your batter!
  5. Scrape down your bowl once more to make sure everything is well combined.
  6. Pour your batter into your cooled crust and spread evenly.

 

Waterbath, Option One

  1. Bring a kettle or saucepan of water to a boil.
  2. Wrap springform pan in two layers of heavy-duty aluminum foil.
  3. Add, and bake your crust per instructions. Add cheesecake filling once cool.
  4. Take your unbaked cheesecake and place in a roasting pan. Transfer your roasting pan to an oven rack.
  5. Pour your boiling water into the roasting pan until it reaches about halfway up the sides of the springform pan.

 

Waterbath, Option Two

  1. Bring a kettle or saucepan of water to a boil.
  2. Transfer a roasting pan to the bottom rack of your oven.
  3. Transfer the unbaked cheesecake to the middle rack of your oven, just above the roast pan.
  4. Carefully pour the boiling water into the roasting pan.

 

Bake

  1. Shut the oven door as soon as you carefully pour the boiling water in your roast pan. We want to keep as much steam in the oven as possible.
  2. Bake at 350Â°f for 60-75 minutes until your cheesecake is slightly puffed and golden. The center of your cheesecake will have a slight jiggle when nudged.
  3. Allow to cool at room temp for two hours.
  4. After two hours, wrap with saran wrap, and pop your cheesecake in the fridge overnight.
  5. After sitting in the fridge overnight, it should be quite easy to remove your cheesecake from the bottom of your springform pan.
  6. Very Gently, wiggle a butter knife between the bottom of your pan and the cheesecake to lift the crust a bit. Then gently lift your cheesecake off of your pan.

 

Toppings

  1. The following morning, prepare your toppings.
  2. Whipped cream: In a mixing bowl with either a stand or hand mixer, whip your whipped cream mixture until soft peaks form.
  3. Jam: In a pot, add your raspberries, sugar and lemon juice. Bring to a boil while continuously mixing with a wooden spoon. Simmer for 20 minutes while continuously mixing to ensure that your jam doesn't burn on the bottom of your pot.
  4. Plop your homemade whipped cream on the top of your cheesecake. Spread evenly.
  5. Spread on a hefty amount of raspberry jam once cooled.
  6. Decorate with some fresh raspberries & top with lemon zest.
  7. Slice into about 8-10 pieces, and you're done!

Enjoy!
